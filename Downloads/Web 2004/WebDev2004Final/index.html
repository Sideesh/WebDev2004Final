Core Concepts

  • Common misunderstanding: "Hindus worship many gods." Most see one divine reality expressed in many forms.
  • Dharma – Righteous living and duty
  • Karma – Law of cause and effect
  • Moksha – Liberation from the cycle of rebirth
  • Atman – The eternal soul within all beings

    Major Deities

    Ganesha

    Remover of Obstacles – The beloved elephant-headed deity, son of Shiva and Parvati. Invoked at the beginning of any new venture, journey, or study. Known as Vighnaharta (obstacle destroyer) and Buddhi Pradayak (bestower of intelligence). His large ears symbolize listening deeply, big head represents wisdom, and small mouth reminds us to speak less. The mouse as his vehicle teaches humility and the ability to reach small places.

    Lakshmi

    Goddess of Wealth & Prosperity – The divine mother who brings both material and spiritual abundance. Born from the churning of the cosmic ocean, she is the consort of Lord Vishnu. Depicted seated on a lotus with gold coins flowing from her hands, she represents purity, generosity, and beauty. Worshipped especially during Diwali, Lakshmi teaches that true wealth comes with dharma and sharing with others.

    Shiva

    The Destroyer & Transformer – Part of the supreme trinity (Trimurti) with Brahma and Vishnu. Known as Mahadeva (Great God), he is the master of meditation, yoga, and cosmic dance (Tandava). His third eye represents wisdom and destruction of evil, the crescent moon shows control over time, and the Ganges flowing from his hair symbolizes purification. As Nataraja, his dance maintains the cosmic order.

    Vishnu

    The Preserver – The protector of the universe who maintains dharma and balance. He descends to earth in various avatars (incarnations) whenever evil grows too strong. His ten main avatars include Rama (ideal king), Krishna (divine teacher), and the coming Kalki. Resting on the cosmic serpent Ananta in the ocean of milk, he represents infinite consciousness and eternal preservation.

    Durga

    The Invincible Warrior Goddess – Created by the combined energies of all gods to defeat the buffalo demon Mahishasura. Riding a lion and wielding divine weapons in her many arms, she represents the triumph of good over evil. During Navratri, she is worshipped in nine forms, embodying feminine power (Shakti). Mother of the universe, protector of the righteous, and destroyer of ego and ignorance.

    Saraswati

    Goddess of Knowledge & Arts – The divine embodiment of learning, music, wisdom, and speech. Consort of Brahma, she plays the veena and holds sacred scriptures and a rosary. Riding a white swan (symbol of discernment), dressed in white for purity, she flows like a river of consciousness. Students pray to her before exams and artists seek her blessings for creativity.

    Hanuman

    Devotion Personified – The ultimate devotee of Lord Rama, son of the wind god. Known for incredible strength, unwavering loyalty, and selfless service. In the Ramayana, he leaps across the ocean, burns Lanka, and carries an entire mountain to save Lakshmana. Symbol of courage, celibacy (brahmacharya), and bhakti (devotion). Chanting his name is believed to remove fear and grant strength.

    Krishna

    The Divine Teacher – The eighth avatar of Vishnu, whose life is celebrated in the Bhagavad Gita and Mahabharata. As a child, he was mischievous and loving; as a youth, the enchanting flute player; as an adult, the wise counselor on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. Teaches karma yoga (path of action), bhakti yoga (path of devotion), and jnana yoga (path of knowledge). "Yada yada hi dharmasya..." – whenever righteousness declines, he returns.
